LGT Obstetric Anaesthesia

Quick Reference Guide

Lower Segment Caesarean Section (LSCS)
Regional Anaesthesia – Spinal
  • 14–16G IV access, warmed fluids running
  • 2.2–2.5 ml 0.5% heavy bupivacaine + 300 mcg diamorphine
  • Phenylephrine infusion 50 mcg/ml
    • Start at 40 ml/hr
    • Aim <10% drop in systolic BP
  • Aim bilateral sensory block T4–S3
Regional Anaesthesia – CSE
  • Consider if prolonged surgery or complex medical conditions
  • Reduce spinal dose in cardiac disease, short stature, obesity
  • Phenylephrine as above
  • Tegaderm + Mefix dressing
Regional Anaesthesia – Epidural Top-Up
  • Only if epidural functioning appropriately
  • 0.5% bupivacaine or 0.75% ropivacaine 15–20 ml in 5 ml increments
  • OR 2% lidocaine + adrenaline 1:200,000
  • Fentanyl 50–100 mcg
  • Diamorphine 3 mg (or PF morphine 3 mg) at end for post-op analgesia
  • Remove epidural unless contraindicated or high risk of return to theatre
LSCS – General Anaesthesia
  • Indications:
    • Category 1 emergency
    • Neuraxial contraindicated or inadequate
  • 30 ml sodium citrate PO
  • RSI with cricoid pressure
  • Videolaryngoscopy recommended (size 7 COETT)
  • Opioid analgesia after delivery ± TAP blocks
LSCS – Post-operative Care
  • Paracetamol 1 g IV/PR
  • Diclofenac 100 mg PR
  • Regular paracetamol and ibuprofen
  • Opioids PRN with anti-emetics
Other Obstetric Procedures
Trial of Forceps
  • Anaesthesia as for LSCS (block to T4)
  • Potential for conversion to LSCS
  • No routine prophylactic antibiotics (discuss with obstetrician)
Manual Removal of Placenta
  • Assess haemodynamic stability
  • If unstable → General anaesthesia
  • If stable → Neuraxial anaesthesia (block to T6)
  • Spinal:
    • 2 ml 0.5% heavy bupivacaine ± fentanyl 15–25 mcg
  • Epidural top-up as alternative
Perineal Tear Repair
  • Sacral block required
  • Epidural top-up:
    • 2% lidocaine + adrenaline OR
    • 0.5% bupivacaine (10 ml)
  • Spinal:
    • 0.5% heavy bupivacaine 1.5–2 ml ± fentanyl
    • Remain sitting ~3 minutes to enhance sacral spread
  • Paracetamol + diclofenac; opioids rarely required
