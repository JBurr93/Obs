Regional Anaesthesia - CSE
  • Consider for prolonged surgery or complex medical conditions
  • Reduce spinal dose in cardiac disease, short stature, obesity
  • Phenylephrine infusion as per spinal
  • Tegaderm dressing + mefix strip
  • If initial spinal bolus does not achieve desired block height, use 5ml bolus of epidural top-ups and re-assess
  • Consider 5-10ml epidural top-up after 75-90 mins if surgery ongoing
Regional anaesthesia - Epidural Top-Up
  • Only if functioning adequately. If not, remove and place spinal
  • Use 1 of the following:
    • 0.5% bupivacaine
    • 0.75% ropivacaine
    • 2% lidocaine + adrenaline 1:200,000 (0.1ml 1:1000 adrenaline drawn up to 20ml with 2% lidocaine)
  • Test dose of 3ml, followed by 5ml increments up to a total of 20ml
  • Add fentanyl 50â€“100 mcg to initial bolus
  • Diamorphine 3 mg (or PF morphine 3 mg) at end

Epidural
  • Contraindications
    • Maternal refusal
    • Clotting abnormalities or thrombocytopenia
      • PLT 75-100x10^9 - proceed if clotting studies normal
      • PLT 50-75x10^9 - consider proceeding if benefits outweight risk - most experienced operator
      • PLT <50x10^9 - epidural contraindicated
    • Local infection at site of insertion
    • Allergy to amide LA
    • Lack of Midwifery staff for 1:1 care
  • Procedure
    • L2/3, L3/4, L4/5
    • 16G Tuohy, LOR to saline
    • Leave 5cm of catheter in the epidural space
    • Routine post-insertion checks (meniscal drop, aspiration)
    • Secure with Lockit, tegaderm and Mefix dressing
    • Test dose 5ml + 10ml loading of low-dose bag mixture (0.1% L-bupivacaine + 2mcg/ml fentanyl)
    • Maintenance infusion via Bodyguard pump
      • Code 611
      • PCEA with 8ml (UHL) or 10ml (QEH) bolus with 20min lockout (max 120ml / 4 hours)
Epidural troubleshooting
  • No block or low block
    • 10-20ml low-dose mix (bag solution) via epiudural, re-check in 20mins
    • If no improvement, resite
  • Missed segment
    • Truly a segment? vs. unilateral block?
    • If confirmed segment, give 7-10ml 0.25% bupivacaine + fentanyl 50mcg
    • Position mother sore side down
    • If no improvement after 20mins, resite
  • Poor sacral coverage
    • Suspect if describing groin, vaginal, rectal or perineal pain
    • Check for S1/2 coverage with spray on lateral aspect of feet
    • Give 10-20ml bag solution with mother sitting upright
    • Recheck after 20mins, if not improved then consider resite (ideally at lower interspace)
  • Unilateral block
    • Check if feet are warm and dry bilaterally - if one sided, does this correspond to the pain?
    • Put patient in lateral position sore side down and administer bolus from pump
    • If no improvement, pull epidural catheter back by 1-2cm and give another bolus from pump
    • If still no improvement, give 7-10ml 0.25% bupivacaine + 50mcg fentanyl whilst sore side down
    • Recheck block after 20mins, if not improved then consider resite
  • Lower back pain/'Rectal pressure'
    • More likely in late stages of labour (8-10cm dilation)
    • Particularly common with OP head position
    • Ensure block is working correctly using cold stick/spray (above T10 and good sacral coverage)
    • Warn mother that complete relief may not be achievable
    • Give 7-10ml 0.25% bupivacaine + 50mcg fentanyl, sit mother upright and assess response after 20mins
PCA
  • Consider if neuraxial analgesia contraindicated or declined
  • Remifentanil PCA under local protocol
  • Requires 1:1 midwifery care and continuous monitoring
