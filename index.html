LGT Obstetric Anaesthesia

Quick Reference Guide

LSCS – Regional Anaesthesia
Spinal – Standard
  • 14–16G IV access, warmed fluids running
  • 2.2–2.5 ml 0.5% heavy bupivacaine + 300 mcg diamorphine
  • Phenylephrine infusion 50mcg/ml started at 40ml/hr
  • Aiming bilateral sensory block T4-S3
CSE
  • Consider for prolonged surgery or patients with complex medical conditions
  • Reduce spinal dose in cardiac disease, short stature, obesity
  • Phenylephrine as above
  • Tegaderm dressing + mefix strip
Epidural Top-Up
  • Only if functioning adequately. If not, remove and place spinal
  • 0.5% bupivacaine or 0.75% ropivacaine 15–20 ml in 5 ml increments
  • OR 2% lidocaine + adrenaline 1:200,000 (0.1ml 1:1000 adrenaline in 20ml 2% lidocaine)
  • Add fentanyl 50–100 mcg
  • Diamorphine 3 mg (or preservative-free morphine 3mg) down epidural at end for post-op analgesia
  • Remove epidural unless contraindicated (e.g. coagulopathy) or high risk of return to theatre
Inadequate analgesia under RA
  • If patient has epidural, a further top up can be given
  • Offer GA, and convert to GA if patient accepts
  • Rescue analgesia includes:
  • Alfentanil 200mcg increments, fentanyl 20mcg increments
  • Ketamine 10-20mg IV
  • Nitrous oxide in O2 (50:50)
  • Clearly document the events and discussions with patient
LSCS – General Anaesthesia
Indications
  • Category 1 emergencies
  • Neuraxial contraindicated
    • Maternal refusal
    • Maternal coagulopathy
    • CVS instability
    • Maternal sepsis/localised infection
  • Regional anaesthesia inadequate for surgery
Management
  • 30ml PO sodium citrate
  • 14-16G cannula with warm fluids running
  • Ramped position with Oxford Help pillow
  • Suction switched on and accessible
  • Peroxygenation:
    • Pre-oxygenate with 100% O2 via facemask
    • Consider HFNO during apnoea
  • When Surgeon is scrubbed and ready, induce:
    • Propofol or Thiopentone
    • Rocuronium or Suxamethonium
    • Alfentanil up to 1mg for hypertensive mothers
  • Videolaryngscopy strongly recommended, size 7 COETT
  • Volatile agent immediately after intubation in 50% N20 at high flows aiming MAC 1.0
  • Opioid analgesia following delivery (10-15mg IV morphine) + PCA
    • Consider TAP blocks if patient consented (20ml 0.25% bupivacaine each side)
LSCS - Post-op care
End of Surgery
  • Paracetamol 1 g IV/PR
  • Diclofenac 100 mg PR
Post-Op Prescription - 'C-section Medication Inpatient Protocol)
  • Paracetamol 1 g QDS
  • Ibuprofen 400 mg TDS–QDS
  • Morphine Sulphate MR 10mg BD for 2 doses
  • Morphine Sulphate IR 10–20 mg 2-hourly PRN
  • Cyclizine 50mg PO TDS
  • Ondansetron 4mg IV TDS
  • Chlorphenamine 4mg PO QDS
  • Naloxone 25mcg SC hourly (max 3 doses)
Labour Analgesia - Epidural
Indications
  • Maternal request for analgesia in labour
Contraindications
  • Maternal refusal
Procedure
  • L2/3, L3/4, L4/5
  • 16G Tuohy, LOR to saline
  • Leave 5cm of catheter in the epidural space
  • Routine post-insertion checks (meniscal drop, aspiration)
  • Secure with Lockit, tegaderm and Mefix dressing
  • Test dose 5ml + 10ml loading of low-dose bag mixture (0.1% L-bupivacaine + 2mcg/ml fentanyl)
  • Maintenance infusion via Bodyguard pump
    • Code 611
    • PCEA with 8ml (UHL) or 10ml (QEH) bolus with 20min interval lockout (max 120ml in 4 hours)
Post-Partum Haemorrhage
Initial Management
  • High-flow O₂
  • 2 large-bore IV lines
  • Warm fluids
  • Activate MHP if >1.5 L
  • TXA 1 g IV
Uterotonics
  • Syntocinon 5 IU IV + infusion
  • Ergometrine 500 mcg IM (avoid PET)
  • Carboprost 250 mcg IM (avoid asthma)
  • Misoprostol 0.4–1 mg PR
